  • Frontend background — React, Redux, TypeScript, Node.js

I build automation frameworks that developers trust, stakeholders can read, and engineers can extend without fear of breaking what already works. I would welcome the opportunity to discuss how my experience aligns with your needs.

Dear Hiring Manager,

Enterprise SAP environments are among the hardest surfaces to automate reliably — authentication layers, SAP GUI scripting quirks, cross-system data dependencies, and compliance requirements that change faster than test suites. I have spent years building automation against exactly this surface, maintaining Robot Framework infrastructure that covers GRC compliance, logistics goods-movement, and user-account integrity across production SAP systems.

Library-Level Engineering

I designed and built CreateXmlFileWaren.py — a custom Python library that reads test data from Excel and generates SAP-structured XML for the Warenverkehr logistics workflow. I extended CommonListener.py to route pass/fail results and suite summaries to Microsoft Teams with KeePass-based credential retrieval — so webhook URLs never appear in source.

GRC Compliance Automation

I own the SAP GRC compliance test suite: automated checks that log into SAP's access-control portal, download risk-check Excel exports, compare them against maintained whitelist datasets, and flag unauthorized critical-role assignments before they become audit findings. These tests run unattended on Jenkins across PS1, PC1, and QV1 systems. A false negative has real audit implications — I treat the suite's accuracy as a production concern.

What I Bring

I bring technical depth at the library level and enough domain knowledge in SAP and GRC to understand what the tests are actually guaranteeing. I am looking for a role where test automation is treated as an engineering discipline — designing frameworks, building tooling, and maintaining the suite as a system worth owning over time. I would be glad to discuss how that applies to your context.
